These aren't arbitrary examples. They're the three industries I know best as a customer, as a business observer, and as someone who has felt the friction firsthand.

I recently renovated my home and watched a capable contractor drown in spreadsheets and phone calls that a simple custom system could have handled. I drop my kids off at childcare every morning and see directors managing enrollment, billing, and compliance from a patchwork of tools that don't talk to each other. The common thread isn't the industry. It's the pattern: capable, growing businesses being underserved by tools that were never really built for them. Technology is accelerating across AI, automation, and real-time data. It's widening the gap between businesses that have custom-built infrastructure and those that don't. These three verticals aren't the limit of what we do. They're where the opportunity is most obvious to me personally.
